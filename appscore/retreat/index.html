This year’s retreat was on Friday, August 22nd at Cavallo Point Lodge, with a dinner the evening before at Murray Circle Restaurant, and ending with a wine reception. The lodge is a spectacular venue, outstanding in every element from rooms to facilities to its breathtaking view of The Golden Gate Bridge.

 

Presentations and Panels

The founding purpose of the App Retreat is to allow leaders and innovators within the app economy to have time to engage in great content—and with each other—on the issues that we face on topics around app distribution, discovery and ecosystem development.

 

The State of the App Economy 2014
Vishal Gurbuxani, Team In Residence

Each year the App Retreat starts with a lay of the land centered on the app economy. Led by Vishal Gurbuxani, we took an in-depth look at the key trends in the app economy: who are the winners and where is there disruption taking place? What trends are driving apps? Who are the ascending and descending players? And what opportunities lie ahead?

The Apps for Everything Revolution
A Roundtable

Apps started with mobile—and now they are about to become a part of everything that we do, from cars to appliances. This will change how apps are screened and how they are purchased.

What lays ahead for apps for everything? Who will be the dominant players? Which OS’s will lead the Apps for Everything revolution? What does an inter-connected world of apps mean for developers? And how will apps work across multiple new surfaces and devices?

These roundtables featured leaders who gave a short presentation on a key challenge that they face, followed by an interactive discussion amongst retreat attendees.

This roundtable was led by QNX Cloud Vice President Alec Saunders and AllSeen Alliance Board of Directors Ryo Koyama. Moderated by Trevor Cornwell, appbackr Founder and CEO.

The Origin and Evolution of the FICO Score
Anthony Sprauve, Senior Consumer Credit Specialist FICO

FICO uses mathematical models in their score to take into account various factors in different areas to determine credit risk. With Anthony Sprauve, the retreat was given a lens toward how scoring as a tenet of their business model is cultivated by the dynamics of the origin and evolution of FICO, how consumers can avail of reliable quality evaluation, and how consumer engagement is key to success.

appscore
Trevor Cornwell, Founder and CEO, appbackr; Vy Nguyen, Vice President, Product & Data, appbackr

appscore is a quality score which drives discovery, distribution, and incentives for developers’ apps. appscore is the foundation of Xchange which provides incentives and rewards to developers. Developers get incentives to port apps and to promote their apps on partner sites operated by carriers, OEMs, and other third parties.

Developers and consumers can learn more at www.appbackr.com/appscore

How Curation, Data, and Quality are Changing the App Landscape
A Roundtable

Hundreds of thousands and millions don’t matter when it comes to apps—people want the apps that are relevant for them. That is why dominant players and startups are looking to unlock the opportunities in discovery.

To uncover the right apps, what is missing? What is the role of search options? How does curation affect downloads? What are the trends for OEMs and carriers when it comes to featuring the apps that will drive consumer interest? Attendees heard from industry experts who are driving the future in the nascent world of app discovery.

This roundtable was led by Mozilla Editorial & Campaign Manager Scott DeVaney, and Samsung Head of US Developer Relations Team Hod Greeley. Moderated by Nathan Beckord, Foundersuite Co-founder and CEO.

The Growth of Mobile Internet Revenue
Tim Merel, Managing Director Digi-Capital

The world of mobile internet, games, and digital investment is navigated by the work in data put forth by firms like Digi-Capital. Managing Director Tim Merel explored how best to profit from the big numbers pulled from research on the growth of mobile internet revenue from varying perspectives of the market.

The Road Ahead – Where is the Money Going?

The App Retreat closed with the people who are making the big bets on mobile. A panel of leading investors, moderated by a journalist who covers venture for Thomson Reuters helped us see where the market is going and what the trends are that affect your business.

The Road Ahead Panelists

Charles Yim e.ventures

Formerly known as BV Capital, the team at e.ventures manages its investments through 11 partners in 5 geographies. A Global Venture Capital Fund, its investment focus is on early stage opportunities from tomorrow’s leading companies in consumer Internet, media, and mobile intersect.

Ben Fu Next World Capital

Next World Capital is an international expansion-stage venture capital firm, investing in technology and software among other groups, and focusing on helping each of its portfolio companies address the challenges and opportunities in building a world-class company.

Josh Breinlinger Sigma West

Sigma West is an early stage technology venture capital firm that is committed to investing in entrepreneurs with passion and drive. Their representative brings to the table a facet of Sigma West that has expertise in online marketing, marketplaces, and crowdsourcing.

Jeff Tannenbaum BlueRun Ventures

BlueRun Ventures is an early stage venture firm focused on mobile opportunities in key global markets, and on working with mobile startups to shape the ever-evolving ecosystem. BlueRun chooses key geographic markets where mobile is a driving force in the way consumers and businesses interact with each other.
