Samsung’s NEW Samsung GALAXY Apps

Featuring the best apps, optimized for Samsung mobile devices and offering users the highest quality experience.

Samsung’s partner appbackr helps discover great apps for the new store. Months before the Samsung GALAXY Apps launched, appbackr began contacting developers with high-scoring apps with invitations to upload their apps to Samsung via appbackr Xchange.

 

How to get your app in the Samsung GALAXY Apps?

We’re now opening the platform to all apps that have been optimized for the newest Samsung devices including the Samsung Galaxy S5®, S4® and Galaxy Note 3® devices.

Samsung GALAXY Apps restricts its selection to only 300 apps ensuring that consumers discover the apps they want and that take full advantage of Samsung devices advanced features. Samsung rotates apps in and out of the store to offer its users the best new apps.

How it works

 

 
  • 1 LOG IN OR SIGN UP
    Log into your appbackr Xchange account. Don't have an account? Sign up today!

  • 3 VIEW OPPORTUNITIES
    Select “Samsung GALAXY Apps” on the Opportunities tab.
  • 2 CLAIM YOUR ANDROID APPS
    Connect your appbackr account with your Android apps by claiming your app on the Overview tab.
  • 4 UPLOAD YOUR APP
    Complete the 3 screens to upload your app to appbackr Xchange.
 

 

appbackr will send the app information to Samsung. If Samsung approves the app, it will be uploaded to the Samsung GALAXY apps store. You’ll receive emails when the app’s status is changed. If Samsung selects the app for the Samsung GALAXY Apps store, appbackr will act as the publisher of the app. All sales will be transferred to the developer.

Your questions answered.

 
Which countries will the Samsung Galaxy Store launch?

Samsung expects to launch in all countries on the first day of the store launch. Developers can set the service countries when they submit the app to appbackr.

Can developers use push ad notifications?

Samsung has a policy regarding push-ad. Please refer to the certification policy on the developer site. As it’s stated in the certification policy, an app built with 'the push ad library' will be rejected in the QA.

Do developers need to change their ad network SDKs?

Developers don't have to change the ad network SDKs. The developer does not have to share advertising revenue with Samsung?

Do the developers have to use the Samsung SDK for IAP?

Embedding Samsung IAP isn't mandatory. They can submit the app with same APK on the Google Play but Samsung offers 80/20 revenue share to those who use Samsung IAP.

You can find the materials for Samsung SDKs including IAP in the Samsung Developer Site.
